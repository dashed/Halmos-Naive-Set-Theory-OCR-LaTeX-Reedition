\begin{equation*}
\cap \{ X : X \in \mathcal{C} \}

\begin{equation*}
\cap_{X \in \mathcal{C}} X.
\end{equation*}

\begin{named}[EXERCISE. ]  A necessary and sufficient condition that $(A \cap B) \cup C = A \cap (B \cup C)$ is that $C \subset A$. Observe that the condition has nothing to do with the set B.
\end{named}
